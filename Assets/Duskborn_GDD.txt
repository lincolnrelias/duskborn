DUSKBORN Game Design Document Co-op Survival Roguelite  |  Unity  |  P2P (1–5 players)  |  Steam Version 0.2  —  Draft   Property Value Genre Co-op Survival Roguelite Platform PC — Steam Engine Unity (URP) Multiplayer P2P — 1 host + up to 4 players (max 5) Netcode FishNet + FishySteamworks transport Art Style Stylized low-poly 3D + pixel art UI Win condition Defeat the Night 7 boss Lose condition All players simultaneously dead V1 scope 1 biome (Forest/Plains), 3 classes, 7 nights Planned biomes 5 total (4 deferred to post-v1) 1. Overview Duskborn is a co-op survival roguelite for up to 5 players. You crash-land on a procedurally generated world, gather resources and open chests during the day, then defend against escalating monster waves each night. Survive 7 nights and defeat the final boss to win the run. Inspired by Muck's simplicity — fast to start, immediate tension, no bloat. Depth comes from class synergies, item builds, and shrines. Meta-progression persists between runs through a class skill tree unlocked in the lobby.  1.1 Design Pillars Every system — classes, crafting, shrines — rewards coordination without making solo unviable.  Co-op first. Crafting choices and item builds should be meaningful. No padding. Every tier changes how you play.  Depth over grind. Procedural generation, random chests, and shrines ensure no two runs play out the same.  Runs feel alive. Ship the core loop clean. Customization, extra biomes, and extra classes come later.  Muck-level simplicity for v1.  1.2 Scope Decisions — Locked for V1 Locked: 3 classes only — Warrior, Mage, Ranger All three available from the start. No class unlocking in v1. Post-v1 roster expansion planned.  Locked: 1 biome — Forest/Plains Single biome fully implemented. The other 4 biomes are designed but deferred.  Locked: Simple flat skill tree — lobby only 5-node flat trees per class. Only accessible in the menu/lobby phase, not mid-run.  Locked: No in-combat revival Allies can revive downed players only when no enemies are nearby. All dead = game over.  Locked: Muck-style simplicity Character customization, deeper biomes, and extra systems are post-v1. Ship the loop first. 2. Game Flow The game follows a tight loop structured around day/night cycles over 7 nights, culminating in a boss fight.  2.1 Session Flow Step Phase Key Actions 1 Main Menu Host creates or joins a lobby. Class selection here. Skill tree editable. Meta-currency shown. 2 Lobby / Pre-run All players see each other's class. Host starts run. Seed generated by host and synced to all clients. 3 Daytime (Days 1–7) Gather resources, open chests (gold cost), visit shrines, craft gear at workbenches. Prepare before the night timer. 4 Nighttime (Nights 1–6) Monster waves converge on players. Monsters drop gold. No chest interaction. Downed players revivable outside combat. 5 Repeat Nights escalate in enemy count, variety, and difficulty each cycle. 6 Night 7 — Boss Biome boss spawns instead of a regular wave. Defeating it wins the run and awards meta-currency. 7 End Screen Run summary: nights survived, damage dealt, gold earned. Meta-currency awarded. Return to menu.   2.2 Day Structure Each day is a timed window (exact duration TBD, estimated 3–5 minutes real time). Players are free to explore, gather, and craft. A UI countdown shows time until nightfall. As days progress, players may choose to push further from the spawn area for better resources at the risk of being caught out when night hits.  2.3 Night Structure Enemies spawn at the edges of the map and converge on living players. The night ends when all enemies are dead or when a timer expires (any remaining enemies despawn at dawn to avoid softlocks). Gold dropped by enemies stays on the ground and can be collected at any time.  2.4 Night Escalation Night New Threat Notes Night 1 Basic swarmers Tutorial wave — low count, slow enemies. Eases players into the loop. Night 2 Runners introduced Faster enemies that target isolated players. Punish poor positioning. Night 3 Spitters introduced Ranged enemies that force players to push out and not camp passively. Night 4 Brutes introduced High-HP slow enemies that soak damage and peel for others. Night 5 Elite enemy appears Named mini-boss tier unit. Higher HP/damage, drops guaranteed gold bonus. Night 6 All types, high density Largest non-boss wave. Good gear check before the final night. Night 7 BOSS FIGHT Biome boss spawns instead of a regular wave. Defeating it completes the run.   2.5 Shrines Shrines are scattered across the map and provide meaningful strategic choices. Two types in v1:  Shrine Type Description Interaction Buff Shrine Grants a passive bonus for the rest of the run (e.g. +15% move speed, lifesteal on hit, +1 item to next chest). One-time interact. No cost. First come, first served in co-op. Challenge Shrine Triggers a timed mini-wave or condition (kill X enemies, survive Y seconds without taking damage). Reward on completion: guaranteed item of Uncommon rarity or higher, or a gold pile. 3. Classes Three classes available in v1. Players choose before each run in the lobby. All classes can gather resources and interact with the world identically — class differences are combat-focused. Skill trees (5 nodes per class) are unlocked between runs using meta-currency. All nodes are available from the start — no prerequisites in v1. The tree is only accessible from the main menu / lobby screen.   Class 01 · Melee Frontliner Warrior Description High HP, strong melee damage, natural frontliner. The class that requires least coordination to play effectively, but rewards aggressive momentum. Best placed at the front of the group during night waves. Passive Each consecutive melee hit on the same enemy increases damage by 8%, stacking up to 3x. Resets on a miss or if the target switches. Active Ability Cleave — Wide arc melee attack hitting all enemies in a 180-degree cone in front. Short cooldown. Primary wave-clearing tool. Skill Tree Nodes Toughness —  +20% max HP Iron Skin —  -10% incoming damage Berserker —  +15% damage when below 40% HP Cleave+ —  Cleave hits briefly stun for 0.5s Lifesteal —  5% HP restored per melee hit   Class 02 · Ranged DPS Ranger Description Medium HP, high single-target ranged damage, fast movement. Best at kiting enemies and eliminating dangerous priority targets from a distance. Needs space to operate effectively. Passive First shot on any new target always critically strikes. Moving for 2 consecutive seconds grants +10% attack speed. Active Ability Rain of Arrows — Brief channel, then fires a wide volley of arrows in a broad arc. High total damage, best used before a wave reaches the group. Skill Tree Nodes Eagle Eye —  +15% critical strike chance Swift —  +15% movement speed Piercing Shot —  Arrows pass through one enemy Quiver+ —  +2 arrows per Rain of Arrows cast Trapper —  Killing blow drops a slowing trap at target location   Class 03 · Magic / AoE Mage Description Low HP, powerful area spells, manages a mana resource. Highest damage ceiling but most punishing when caught in melee. Requires team protection and positioning discipline to shine. Passive Killing an enemy with a spell restores a small amount of mana. Critical spell hits apply a random status effect: Burn, Freeze, or Shock. Active Ability Arcane Burst — Releases a short-range AoE explosion centered on the Mage. High damage in a radius. Panic button when surrounded. Skill Tree Nodes Mana Well —  +25% maximum mana Overload —  +20% spell damage Frost Nova —  Arcane Burst now also freezes all hit enemies briefly Efficiency —  -15% mana cost on all spells Chain —  Damaging spells jump to one additional nearby enemy   3.1 Co-op Synergies Classes are designed to complement each other naturally without enforcing rigid roles: Warrior + Mage — Warrior occupies enemies in melee, giving Mage safe ground to cast high-damage AoEs into clustered groups. Warrior + Ranger — Warrior draws and holds aggro, Ranger kites the overflow and picks off priority targets. Ranger + Mage — Both are fragile; they need each other's crowd control (Mage Freeze, Ranger traps) to survive without a frontliner. All three together — Ideal composition. Warrior holds, Ranger peels flanks, Mage clears clusters. 4. Enemies & Boss 4.1 Enemy Archetypes (V1 — Forest/Plains) Type HP Speed Role First Appears Swarmer Low Medium High-count wave filler. Die to AoE. Primary pressure unit. Night 1 Runner Medium Very Fast Targets isolated players. Punish poor positioning. Night 2 Spitter Low-Med Slow Ranged attacker. Forces players to stay mobile. Night 3 Brute High Slow Soaks damage, opens space for other enemies. Night 4 Elite Very High Medium Named mini-boss unit. Guaranteed gold drop. Night 5   4.2 Revival Rules State Description Downed HP reaches 0 → player enters a downed state (immobile, can't fight). A countdown timer begins. Revive condition A teammate can interact with the downed player ONLY if no enemies are within a set radius around the downed player. This forces the team to clear enemies before reviving. Death If the countdown expires before revival, the player fully dies and is out of the run. Game Over If the last standing player goes down, no one can revive. Run ends immediately. Partial reward Even on game over, partial meta-currency is awarded based on nights survived.   4.3 Night 7 Boss — Thornbark Boss — Thornbark, the Corrupted Grove A massive corrupted treant. Two-phase fight. Drops: run completion + full meta-currency reward.  Phase 1 (100%–50% HP) Stomp — AoE ground slam in a radius around Thornbark. Telegraphed with a dust ring. Deals heavy damage. Vine Whip — Targets a single player with a long-range vine strike. Fast but telegraphed by a red indicator. Root Eruption — Random ground spikes erupt across the arena. Players must dodge the spread pattern.  Phase 2 (below 50% HP) All Phase 1 attacks remain, now faster cadence. Seedling Swarm — Continuously spawns swarmer-tier seedlings. Must be cleared or they overwhelm the team. Thorn Barrage — Wide thorn eruption pattern covering most of the arena. Large telegraph window but punishing if caught.  Co-op mechanic Players must split attention between clearing the seedling adds (or they pile up) and maintaining DPS on Thornbark. Ignoring either fails the fight — a natural division of roles between classes. 5. Loot & Crafting 5.1 Gold Economy (In-Run) Gold is the primary in-run currency. It does NOT persist between runs and resets at the start of each run.  Source Amount Notes Basic enemy kill 1–3 gold Scales slightly with night number. Elite enemy kill 10–20 gold Guaranteed drop. Boss kill Large pile (TBD) Run completion bonus. Challenge shrine Variable Depends on challenge difficulty.   Sink Cost Notes Basic chest Low gold cost (TBD) Rolls 1 item at Common–Uncommon rarity. Advanced chest Higher gold cost (TBD) Rolls 1 item at Uncommon–Rare rarity. Rarer chests found deeper in the map. Legendary chest High gold cost (TBD) Rolls 1 item at Rare–Legendary rarity.   5.2 Chest Item Rarity (Risk of Rain-inspired) Items found in chests roll a rarity at the moment of opening. Rarity determines stat power and effect complexity. Items stack — you can hold multiple copies of the same item and effects accumulate.  Rarity Color Effect style Drop rate Common Gray Simple flat stat boosts — +HP, +speed, +damage %. Stack well and form the foundation of any build. High Uncommon Green Conditional effects — 'On kill, gain movement speed for 3s.' Moderate impact, good for building momentum. Medium Rare Blue Build-defining passives — 'Every 5th hit fires a free projectile.' Fundamentally changes the combat rhythm. Low Legendary Pink Powerful run-altering effects. Very impactful — finding one reshapes how you play the rest of the run. Very Low Cursed Amber Strong upside + meaningful downside — '+50% damage, -30% max HP.' Deliberate risk/reward. Player chooses to take it. Rare   Key distinction: Chest items vs. Crafted gear Items from chests are passive bonuses that stack (Risk of Rain-style). Crafted gear (weapon, armor, accessory) replaces the previous tier — only one of each equipped at a time. These are two parallel systems, not competing ones.   5.3 Crafting — Weapons, Armor, Accessories Crafting is done at workbenches built at the base. Only resources are required — no gold. Crafting produces the primary power progression path; chest items are build flavor on top.  Crafting Tiers (V1 — Forest/Plains) Tier Weapon example Armor example Key resource Tier 1 — Primitive Wooden Sword, Stone Bow, Oak Staff Leather Armor set Wood, Stone, Fiber Tier 2 — Iron Iron Sword, Iron Bow, Iron Wand Iron Armor set Iron Ore (mined) Tier 3 — Reinforced (post-boss) Reinforced Sword, Hunter's Bow, Arcane Staff Reinforced Armor set Thornbark Core (boss drop) + Iron Tier 4+ Planned for post-v1 biomes — Boss-gated rare materials   Crafted Slots Weapon — Determines basic attack type and stat scaling. One equipped at a time. Replaced by higher tier. Armor — Head, Chest, Legs (or single armor rating — TBD). Crafted from resources. One set tier active. Accessory — Ring, amulet, or trinket. One equipped at a time. Small targeted bonus (e.g. Stone Ring: +5% crit).   5.4 Status Effects Effect Source Gameplay impact Burn Mage crits, fire items Damage over time. Spreads to nearby enemies if stacked. Freeze Mage Frost Nova skill, ice items Briefly immobilizes target. Shatters on next hit for bonus damage. Shock Mage crits, lightning items Temporarily reduces enemy armor/resistance. Slow Ranger Trapper skill, trap items Reduces movement speed. Makes kiting safer. Stun Warrior Cleave+ skill Briefly interrupts enemy action. Applies to all Cleave targets. 6. Meta-Progression Meta-progression persists between runs and is stored locally per player. It cannot be accessed mid-run — only from the main menu and lobby screen.  6.1 Meta-Currency Earned at the end of each run. Working name: Echoes. A completed run (boss defeated) awards the full amount. An early game over awards a partial amount proportional to nights survived.  Outcome Echoes rewarded Win (boss defeated) Full award (exact value TBD during balancing) Game over — Night 6 ~80% of full award Game over — Night 4–5 ~50% of full award Game over — Night 2–3 ~25% of full award Game over — Night 1 Minimal / token amount   6.2 Skill Trees Each class has a 5-node flat skill tree. All nodes are available to purchase in any order — no prerequisites in v1. Each node has a fixed Echo cost. Once purchased, the node is permanently unlocked for all future runs with that class. Class unlocks — Future classes unlocked via Echoes. Cosmetics — Character skins, building material themes. Purely visual, no gameplay impact. Hard mode modifiers — Opt-in challenge flags for bonus Echo rewards (permadeath, no base building, etc.). 7. World & Map 7.1 V1 Biome — Forest/Plains The Forest/Plains biome is the only biome in v1. It is procedurally generated each run using a host-generated seed distributed to all clients for deterministic world recreation.  Feature Description Terrain Open grassy plains interspersed with dense forest clusters. Rivers and rock formations as natural barriers. Resources Wood (trees), Stone (rocks), Fiber (grass/bushes), Iron Ore (stone outcroppings, rarer nodes) Points of interest Workbench sites, shrine locations, chest spawn points — placed by the generator using hand-authored prefab chunks at seeded positions. Spawn zone Players always spawn in a central clearing — safe, flat ground, with a workbench nearby. Enemy spawn Enemies spawn at the map's perimeter during night waves and path toward players.   7.2 Planned Biomes (Post-V1) Biome Theme Status Forest/Plains Starting biome — lush, temperate. Multiplayer Architecture 8.1 Network Model All multiplayer is peer-to-peer. One player acts as host; up to 4 others join via Steam invite or lobby code. There are no dedicated servers. The host machine runs simulation authority. Clients send inputs; the host resolves outcomes and syncs world state back to clients. The host also generates the world seed.  8.2 Netcode — FishNet Selected: FishNet (Fish-Networking) Free, MIT license. Modern API designed for Unity. Host-authority model built in. FishySteamworks transport provides direct Steam Relay integration for NAT punch-through without requiring Unity Gaming Services.  Library Pros Cons Verdict FishNet Free · MIT · Modern clean API · Host-authority native · FishySteamworks transport · Active dev Smaller community than Mirror · Younger project Selected Mirror Free · MIT · Largest community · Very stable · Years of production use · Steamworks transport Older API style · Slightly lower perf than FishNet Runner-up Unity NGO Official Unity support Relay is paid past free tier · Least mature · Most complex P2P setup Not recommended   8.3 Multiplayer Design Rules Shared world, shared base — All players share a single resource pool and base. Coordination is required for crafting and building decisions. Individual class and skill tree — Each player picks their own class and has their own unlocked skill tree nodes. Solo runs are fully supported. Death and revival — See Section 4.2. No in-combat revival; all-dead = game over. Player count scaling — Enemy HP, count, and damage scale with player count. Resource yields may also scale to avoid starvation in 5-player lobbies. Exact formula TBD during balancing. Late join — Disabled in v1. Joining a run in progress is not supported. Players must join before the host starts the run. 9. Art Direction 9.1 3D Style — Stylized Low-Poly The 3D world uses a stylized low-poly aesthetic: clean geometry, minimal surface detail, flat or lightly shaded colors. Think Muck, early low-poly indie games. Low vertex count keeps performance strong and artist workload manageable, especially during the scope of v1.  9.2 UI Style — Pixel Art All HUD elements, menus, item icons, skill tree UI, and inventory use pixel art. This creates a deliberate and distinctive dual-aesthetic — the 3D world is low-poly while the interface feels like a classic RPG. Strong visual contrast makes UI readable even during combat.  UI Element Style Item icons Pixel art at 16x16 or 32x32 (consistent resolution TBD — decide early and enforce) HUD (HP/mana bars) Chunky pixel blocks, not smooth bars Inventory grid Pixel art panel with cell borders Skill tree Pixel art nodes and connector lines Fonts Pixel font throughout all UI Menu / lobby screens Pixel art framing and buttons   9.3 Rendering Pipeline Unity URP (Universal Render Pipeline). Lightweight, good performance headroom, supports custom stylized shaders. A flat/cel shader on character and environment meshes reinforces the low-poly look and keeps the render coherent.  9.4 Color Palette & Mood State Palette Intent Daytime Warm greens, bright yellows, clear blues Safety and calm. Players should feel relaxed while gathering. Nighttime Deep blues, desaturated purples, reduced visibility Threat and tension. The color shift reinforces the day/night stakes. Boss fight (Night 7) Dark red ambient, heavy particle effects Dramatic finale. Signals 'this is the climax' visually. 10. Technical Stack System Technology Notes Engine Unity (LTS) Use current LTS version. URP rendering pipeline. Netcode FishNet + FishySteamworks P2P host-authority. Steam Relay for NAT punch-through. Distribution Steam Steamworks SDK for lobbies, invites, achievements. Proc gen Custom — seed-based Clients generate identical worlds from the host's seed. RNG calls must be deterministic. Persistence Local save (per player) Meta-progression stored locally. Steam Cloud sync TBD for cross-machine saves. Audio TBD FMOD or Unity built-in. Adaptive music for day/night/boss transitions preferred. Shader Custom URP cel/flat shader Applied to all 3D meshes for consistent low-poly aesthetic.   10.1 Key Technical Risks Risk: P2P latency & desync (High) Host-authority with 5 players and real-time combat is the hardest part of the project. Needs early prototyping and a clear decision on interpolation and lag compensation strategy before building gameplay systems on top of it.  Risk: Deterministic proc gen (Medium) All clients must generate an identical world from the same seed. RNG calls must be sequenced identically on every machine. Test this early with a standalone seed validation step.  Risk: Night wave performance (Medium) Pathfinding and entity count during dense waves (Night 6 especially) may stress frame rate. NavMesh baking strategy and entity pooling need to be architected before building the enemy system. 11. Open Questions & TBDs Items still to be resolved during development.
